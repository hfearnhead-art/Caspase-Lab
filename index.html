Caspases in Death and Differentiation

The Caspase Lab, led by Howard Fearnhead, PhD, investigates how caspases and apoptosome signalling control cell death, non-lethal remodelling, and toxicant responses in mammalian cells.

Research

We study how caspases and apoptosome signalling shape cell fate decisions in apoptosis, differentiation, and toxicant responses. Our work combines biochemistry, cell biology, high-content imaging, proteomics, and computational modelling.

Caspases in Apoptosis & Myotoxicity

We dissect how initiator and effector caspases are activated during apoptosis, how they assemble on the apoptosome, and how this contributes to tissue-specific toxicities, including drug-induced myotoxicity. Approaches include cell-free systems, and quantitative Cell Painting assays.

Non-Apoptotic Caspase Functions

Caspases also have essential non-lethal roles in differentiation and nervous system development. We map caspase cleavage events in differentiating cells and neurons to understand how limited proteolysis remodels the cytoskeleton and signalling networks without triggering full apoptosis.

Systems Toxicology & AI

We integrate Cell Painting, transcriptomics and proteomics to predict toxicant-induced cytotoxicity and to infer protease network activity from phenotypic profiles. This informs safer drug design and mechanistic toxicology.
